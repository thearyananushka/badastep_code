The top Law colleges in India include NLSIU Bangalore, NLU Delhi, and NALSAR Hyderabad. IIT Kharagpur, NUJS Kolkata, and NLU Jodhpur are some of the best LLB colleges in India. NLSIU Bangalore is the top Law College in India and is ranked 1 by NIRF. Admissions to top Law colleges are based on Law Entrance Exams such as CLAT, AILET, LSAT, and DU LLB. The top private Law Colleges include SLS Pune, KIIT Bhubaneshwar, and Parul University. The average course fees for pursuing Law courses from top Law Colleges range between INR 10,000 - 18,00,000 against which candidates are placed across top law firms with an average salary of INR 8.33 LPA - 10 LPA. In India, Law is one of the most pursued courses for the numerable scope it offers. There was a staggering 2.74 lakh registrations for just CLAT for 2023 of which 1.99 lakh students appeared for the exam. There are around 1372 Law colleges in India, of which 966 are Private and 369 are Government Law colleges. 642 Law colleges are approved by BCI and 567 are approved by UGC. 1022 Law colleges in India offer LLB courses.
